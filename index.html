Part 1: Preparation

Selected Interface:

The “Maine Root” drink machine in RISD's Met dining hall

Questions:

  1. How frequently do you use these particular drink machines, and are you a RISD student?
  2. How did the experience of using this machine compare to your experience with other drink dispensing machines?
  3. How and when did you decide which drink you were going to select?
  4. What factors led to you selecting the machine and ice dispenser that you did (since there are multiple)?

Sketch & Description:

There are two nearly identical drink machines in the Met that stand on opposite sides of the "drink" corner of the Met. The corner has a stack of clean, empty cups on the left side.
The Maine Root drink machines dispense cubed ice and a variety of soft drinks such as cola, diet cola, ginger ale, and lemonade. The dispensers work by pressing a cup against a vertical, plastic bar which will dispense the corresponding substance for the duration that the bar is pushed. The ice is dispensed in a similar way but with a wider, square bar to press into.

The problem this interface is solving is the issue of allowing users complete control over their soft drink. The goal of this machine is to allow users to easily select and dispense their drink without hassle.

Part 2: Recording Observations

Key Observations:

  • All three observed users observed walked quickly and confidently up to the cups and the machines
  • Two of the users had no hesitation in filling their cups. One user dumped their drink out and got a different one, but they had little hesitation about the change
  • All 3 of the observed got a combination of drinks, at least 2 mixed together. This is not true of all users.
  • The only user who used the machine on the right did so when there was someone currently using the left machine
  • Overall, the users did not visibly struggle with this interface

Question responses:

Question: Response summary:
1. How frequently do you use these particular drink machines, and are you a RISD student?
  • User 1 used the machine less than once per week; is not a RISD student (is here with a friend)
  • User 2 used the machine 5-7 times per week, variably; is a RISD student (Junior)
  • User 3 used the machine 14 times per week, for every meal in the Met; is a RISD student (Junior)
2. How did the experience of using this machine compare to your experience with other drink dispensing machines?
  • User 1 liked that the machine had the plastic push-levers instead of buttons they needed to push
  • They explained this was because it was a "one-handed experience" which they enjoyed
  • User2 only had comments about the the drinks themselves, no issues or comments with the functionality of the machine.
  • User 3 commented that it was nice to have a self-serving machine because they could get "whacky combos" of their choosing
3. How and when did you decide which drink you were going to select?
  • User 1 did not think about which drink they would be getting until they were close enough to see flavors
  • They picked orange soda, then changed their mind when they thought about the flavor and changed to lemonade and seltzer.
  • User 2 decided when they picked their cup up, craving ginger ale and tea for their sore throat instead of their usual water.
  • User 3 said they put their cup in the lemonade by muscle memory or force of habit
  • User 3 added a bunch of different drinks because they have "combos" or "recipes" like this in their head
4. What factors led to you selecting the machine and ice dispenser that you did (since there are multiple)?
  • User 1 said that they used the machine on the right because it had fewer dispensers, seemed less overwhelming, and was less crowded in the flow of traffic
  • User 2, an experienced user, selected the left drink machine and the leftmost ice dispenser because the right side dispenser was "scary"
  • Users 2 and 3 both mentioned that the right machine and ice dispenser were broken last year, so they avoided it, even though it was now fixed
  • User 3 also used the left drink dispenser, mostly since the left one was right next to the cups and had more options

Part 3: Personas

Empathy maps:

"Leisurely Larry"

Interface problems:

Leisurely Larry does not face a lot of problems with this interface. They are perhaps somewhat frustrated by the machine not working, but they have the spirit of moving on and getting a drink anyways. This makes them a fairly good persona, since most drink machine users are frequent users like this who know how to reach their goals even if a few small things go wrong.

"Nervous Nelly"

Interface problems:

Nervous Nelly represents a user base who are less familiar with these particular machines and their quirks. Being less familiar with the space, they may be a little more frustrated when things go wrong. However, we see in Nelly's user journey that avoiding confrontation by going with the flow is what will likely happen if this user encounters any issues. This makes sense since we hardly see users who are genuinely causing a scene.
